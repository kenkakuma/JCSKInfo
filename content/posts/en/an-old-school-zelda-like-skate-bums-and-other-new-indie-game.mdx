Welcome to our latest roundup of what's going on in the indie game space. As a reminder, the latest edition of Steam Next Fest runs from February 23 until March 2, during which you'll be able to check out demos for hundreds of upcoming games. A bunch are available already, including one for [_Denshattack!_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/denshattack-is-a-blend-of-tony-hawk-trains-and-shonen-anime-174106792.html), which I definitely recommend [checking out](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2524850/Denshattack/). As it turns out, doing Tony Hawk Pro Skater-style tricks with a high-speed Japanese train absolutely rips.

On Thursday, there were four showcases highlighting indie games all in a single day. It's not exactly feasible for me to recap them in full here, unfortunately, but I can at least tell you about a few of the many highlights.

The Black History Month edition of the Black Voices in Gaming Showcase includes trailers and interviews for some games that are already available, such as [_Space Warlord Baby Trading Simulator_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/highguard-a-hyperpop-arena-shooter-and-other-new-indie-games-worth-checking-out-120000874.html), [_Aerial\_Knight's DropShot_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/xbox/kingdom-come-deliverance-2-and-the-witcher-3-are-coming-to-game-pass-163624685.html) and [_Relooted_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/heist-game-relooted-gets-a-release-date-173456541.html). Of course, the stream featured plenty of games that are on the way too.

[_Erased_](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3121890/Erased/), from solo developer Jerron Jacques, looks pretty interesting. It's an open-world fighting game that takes place in a cyberpunk setting with dance battles, parkour, pets, strange creatures and much more. Jacques, who has been documenting the game's development process on social media, even [carried out](https://www.instagram.com/p/DUHn8JHDNJW/?hl=en) some of the parkour motion capture work personally.

There was lots of good stuff in this week's Convergence Showcase too, including another peek at _Mouse: P.I. for Hire_ as we get to see one of the game's bosses for the first time. This first-person shooter with rubber-hose animation is [set to arrive on March 19](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/mouse-pi-for-hire-arrives-in-march-2026-195454228.html).

There were other welcome announcements for me in this showcase. First, there was a release date for the Zelda-inspired adventure _Gecko Gods_. I've had this on my wishlist [since 2022](https://www.engadget.com/sports-story-nintendo-switch-eshop-184541324.html/1000), so I'm glad to learn it'll hit Nintendo Switch, PS5 and PC on April 16. 

In addition, record shop sim _Wax Heads_ (which probably should have been called Low Fidelity, tbh) is coming to PC, Xbox Series X/S, PS5, and Nintendo Switch on May 5. You can check out a [Steam Next Fest demo](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2769240/Wax_Heads/) for that one now.

The second edition of Indie Fan Fest had a trove of goodies as well. For one thing, _Balatro_ publisher Playstack could be about to cause another mass reduction in collective productivity with _Raccoin_, which now has a release date of March 31. This is another roguelike deckbuilder, but this time it's in the form of a coin pusher. I didn't have a chance to check out the previous playtest to get a better sense of why there's so much buzz around this one, but I'll for sure be trying the Steam Next Fest demo, which is [available now](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3784030/RACCOIN_Coin_Pusher_Roguelike/).

It remains deeply weird to refer to a game under Acclaim's umbrella as [an indie](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/cozy-detectives-urban-disc-golf-and-other-new-indie-games-worth-checking-out-110029695.html), but that's where we are now. The publisher is bringing Ridiculous Games' _GridBeat_ to Nintendo Switch and Steam on March 26. This is a rhythm-based dungeon crawler in which you (a hacker) try to escape from a corporate network after pilfering valuable data. There's a [Next Fest demo available](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3243370/GRIDbeat/) for this one too. 

Meanwhile, a narrower release window for Japanese convenience store sim [_InKonbini: One Store. Many Stories_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/old-school-platforming-metroidvania-horror-and-other-new-indie-games-worth-checking-out-120000751.html) was revealed. It's coming to [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2723430/inKONBINI_One_Store_Many_Stories/), PS5, Nintendo Switch and Xbox in April.

Alongside a [related sale on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/sale/quebec2026?tab=1), the first Quebec Games Celebration Showcase took place on Thursday. It's always neat to learn about games being made in my neck of the woods.

One of those is _Surfpunk_, a co-op action RPG that looks a bit like _Hades_ with surfing. Radical. You'll venture to procedurally generated islands in search of loot. There are four weapon classes to choose from and gadgets you can craft after collecting resources on your run. There's an [updated Steam demo](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2434830/Surfpunk/) that's said to have around five hours of gameplay. _Surfpunk_ (which is from [_Convergence: A League of Legends Story_](https://www.engadget.com/convergence-a-league-of-legends-story-will-arrive-on-may-23rd-165600767.html) developer Double Stallion) will arrive later this year.

I'm including this [demo announcement](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4408590/Croak_Demo/) trailer for _Croak_, a precision platformer from Woodrunner Games that appears to be heavily inspired by the likes of Celeste, separately for one main reason. You _have_ to check out the studio's head of "barketing." (Okay okay, the game's hand-drawn visuals look lovely too.)

There's plenty of other interesting stuff in the Quebec Games Celebration Showcase, including another look at _Tears of Metal_ from Paper Cult Games, the studio behind the very enjoyable _Bloodroots_. There's a [Steam demo](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1913120/Tears_of_Metal/) available for the hack-and-slash roguelite, which should be out this spring. Gothic sci-fi Metroidvania [_Silent Planet_](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2742920/Silent_Planet__Elegy_of_a_Dying_World/) looks quite tasty too.

## New releases

_Under The Island_ looks and sounds very _The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past_\-coded. But since that's my favorite game of all time, I am not complaining. I love that protagonist Nia appears to use a hockey stick as a weapon too. 

This action PRG from Slime King Games (and co-publishers Top Hat Studios and Doyoyo Games) has debuted to [strong early reviews](https://www.metacritic.com/game/under-the-island/). It's available now on [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1583520/Under_The_Island/), [Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=5f41950e-8ca3-4481-8466-a22b28b80e32&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=ca95510e-a218-4436-8d35-bec59f643ffa&featureId=text-link&merchantName=Xbox&linkText=Xbox+One%2C+Xbox+Series+X%2FS&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy54Ym94LmNvbS9lbi1VUy9nYW1lcy9zdG9yZS91bmRlci10aGUtaXNsYW5kLzlQMFNCRFg0U0hQMiIsImNvbnRlbnRVdWlkIjoiY2E5NTUxMGUtYTIxOC00NDM2LThkMzUtYmVjNTlmNjQzZmZhIiwib3JpZ2luYWxVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy54Ym94LmNvbS9lbi1VUy9nYW1lcy9zdG9yZS91bmRlci10aGUtaXNsYW5kLzlQMFNCRFg0U0hQMiJ9&signature=AQAAAQpbJC1H9nGBUX1Fl-3nBNUUHp7zdhfIrRXxbwhHQLbY&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.xbox.com%2Fen-US%2Fgames%2Fstore%2Funder-the-island%2F9P0SBDX4SHP2), [PS4, PS5](https://store.playstation.com/en-us/concept/10016637) and [Nintendo Switch](https://www.nintendo.com/us/store/products/under-the-island-switch/) for $20, though there's a 15 percent launch sale (you'll need to be a PS Plus member to get the discount on PS4 and PS5).

_Demon Tides —_ a 3D, open-world platformer from _Bubsy 4D_ and _Demon Turf_ developer Fabraz — has lots of movement mechanics, including paragliders and hookshots. You can shapeshift into different forms as well.

You can create and share graffiti, and this will appear in other players' games (which is a neat touch). _Demon Tides_ is out now on [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2585890/Demon_Tides/). It'll usually cost $25, but until March 5, you can snap it up for $20.

_Skate Bums_ is a 2D skateboarding game in the tradition of the OlliOlli series. As novice skateboarder Lux, you'll try to take down the Skate Bums, a gang of bullies. There are "weird characters," sick combos to pull off, coins to collect and wrecking balls to dodge. 

There's said to be a "simple directional trick system" while each run is apparently short. That seems ideal for quick, pick-up-and-play sessions on Nintendo Switch and Switch 2. I also just really like the title. [_Skate Bums_](https://www.nintendo.com/us/store/products/skate-bums-switch/)_,_ which is from Lucky Last Studio, will normally cost $15 but there's a 15 percent discount until February 27.

_Love Eternal_ is a psychological "horror platformer about escaping the domain of a selfish god," which is a strong pitch from developer brlka and _Demonschool_ publisher Ysbryd Games. You'll need to flip between different gravitational pulls as you navigate this precision platformer, which follows teenager Maya on her attempt to return to her own reality. 

During a dinner at home, Maya's family disappears and she suddenly appears in an "eerie, desolate realm" that looks like an Iron Age castle. That's a creepy enough set up to match the game's haunting atmosphere. _Love Eternal_ is out now on [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3010610/LOVE_ETERNAL/), [PS4](https://store.playstation.com/en-us/product/UP2251-CUSA52815_00-0631046602862782), [PS5](https://store.playstation.com/en-us/concept/10013641), [Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=5f41950e-8ca3-4481-8466-a22b28b80e32&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=ca95510e-a218-4436-8d35-bec59f643ffa&featureId=text-link&merchantName=Xbox&linkText=Xbox+One%2C+Xbox+Series+X%2FS&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy54Ym94LmNvbS9lbi1VUy9nYW1lcy9zdG9yZS9sb3ZlLWV0ZXJuYWwvOW5kOHRiaHR3azcyIiwiY29udGVudFV1aWQiOiJjYTk1NTEwZS1hMjE4LTQ0MzYtOGQzNS1iZWM1OWY2NDNmZmEiLCJvcmlnaW5hbFVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vd3d3Lnhib3guY29tL2VuLVVTL2dhbWVzL3N0b3JlL2xvdmUtZXRlcm5hbC85bmQ4dGJodHdrNzIifQ&signature=AQAAAWlWQDikZBqdCrEqZKH0kn--RI3pHFVhhri5EOpOpCFx&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.xbox.com%2Fen-US%2Fgames%2Fstore%2Flove-eternal%2F9nd8tbhtwk72) and [Nintendo Switch](https://www.nintendo.com/us/store/products/love-eternal-switch/) for $10. There's a 15 percent launch discount (again, you'll need to be a PS Plus member to take advantage of that on PlayStation).

## Upcoming 

Woe Industries, the developer of [_You Have Billions Invested In Generative AI_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/hotline-miami-meets-football-the-power-of-video-editing-and-other-new-indie-games-worth-checking-out-120000628.html) (and a bunch of other neat projects), has something intriguing on deck for next week — a standardized gaming test. You can start the [Adventure Game Aptitude Test (AGAT)](https://theagat.co/) at any time between 1PM and 2PM ET on February 28. 

You'll have four hours to complete an '80s adventure game of Woe Industries’ choosing. The developer will seemingly be monitoring your browser and smartphone activity to ensure you don't consult a walkthrough. If you're successful, you'll receive an AGAT certification and diploma. Good luck!

"Musical narrative adventure" _People of Note_ is coming to [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1626170/People_of_Note/), [Epic Games Store](https://store.epicgames.com/en-US/p/people-of-note-e50325), the [Xbox App on PC](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=5f41950e-8ca3-4481-8466-a22b28b80e32&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=ca95510e-a218-4436-8d35-bec59f643ffa&featureId=text-link&merchantName=Xbox&linkText=Xbox+App+on+PC&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy54Ym94LmNvbS9lbi1VUy9nYW1lcy9zdG9yZS9wZW9wbGUtb2Ytbm90ZS85TkIyVEYyUDAxMUIiLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6ImNhOTU1MTBlLWEyMTgtNDQzNi04ZDM1LWJlYzU5ZjY0M2ZmYSIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueGJveC5jb20vZW4tVVMvZ2FtZXMvc3RvcmUvcGVvcGxlLW9mLW5vdGUvOU5CMlRGMlAwMTFCIn0&signature=AQAAAUXfxaUr8XZVJNuI-QbumJXKAsjEdJIMla6JFQ_DyEnj&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.xbox.com%2Fen-US%2Fgames%2Fstore%2Fpeople-of-note%2F9NB2TF2P011B), Nintendo Switch 2, [PS5](https://store.playstation.com/en-us/concept/10014972/) and [Xbox Series X/S](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=5f41950e-8ca3-4481-8466-a22b28b80e32&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=ca95510e-a218-4436-8d35-bec59f643ffa&featureId=text-link&merchantName=Xbox&linkText=Xbox+Series+X%2FS&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy54Ym94LmNvbS9lbi1VUy9nYW1lcy9zdG9yZS9wZW9wbGUtb2Ytbm90ZS85TkIyVEYyUDAxMUIiLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6ImNhOTU1MTBlLWEyMTgtNDQzNi04ZDM1LWJlYzU5ZjY0M2ZmYSIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueGJveC5jb20vZW4tVVMvZ2FtZXMvc3RvcmUvcGVvcGxlLW9mLW5vdGUvOU5CMlRGMlAwMTFCIn0&signature=AQAAAUXfxaUr8XZVJNuI-QbumJXKAsjEdJIMla6JFQ_DyEnj&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.xbox.com%2Fen-US%2Fgames%2Fstore%2Fpeople-of-note%2F9NB2TF2P011B) on April 7 for $25 (though there'll be a 10 percent launch discount). The game follows pop singer Cadence on her quest for stardom. Along the way, Cadence and her buds will have to overcome enemies in battles that have turn-based and rhythm-based elements.

I mostly enjoyed [playing through a preview](https://www.engadget.com/hades-2-slot-machine-horror-and-other-new-indie-games-worth-checking-out-110000884.html) several months ago, though I had to grit my teeth through the turn-based combat, which is not something I enjoy as a rule. However, Iridium Studios will allow players to turn off elements like that and environmental puzzles so more people who might have otherwise been turned off can enjoy _People of Note_. You can sample the game now on Steam via [a 90-minute Next Fest demo](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1626170/People_of_Note/).

I don't fully understand what's going on in the reveal trailer for _Titanium Court_, which is from AP Thomson (a solo developer who previously worked on _Consume Me_) and publisher Fellow Traveller. Even the press release notes that it's "impossibly difficult to describe." But it has an absorbing trailer soundtracked by an odd, Bill Callahan-esque song and it has already picked up [several IGF award nominations](https://igf.com/article/baby-steps-strolls-to-lead-with-5-nominations-at-2026-igf-awards/), so I'm intrigued.

What I am able to surmise is that it's a surrealist, roguelike strategy game with match-three, auto battle and tower defense elements. It's also for "clowns and criminals," apparently. I'm gonna have to give the [Steam Next Fest demo](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2364580/Titanium_Court/) a shot to try to get my head around it. _Titanium Court_ is coming to Steam “imminently.”

Sometimes, a game pops up that makes me think "how has no one done this before?" Such is the case with _Become_. It's a third-person linear adventure from solo developer Valentin Wirth in which you take on the guise of a single sperm. You can probably guess what the goal is.

The game has "no explicit sexual acts, nudity or violence," according to its Steam page, though you will encounter some danger along your journey. You can upgrade your bespectacled spermatozoon via skill trees and seemingly adorn various pieces of headgear. _Become_ is slated to hit [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3710850/Become/) later this year.
