There may have been some extra incentive for the Trump administration to get the [TikTok US deal](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/tiktok-finalizes-deal-for-its-us-entity-010543484.html) done. According to a report from [_The Wall Street Journal_](https://www.wsj.com/tech/tiktok-deal-fee-trump-administration-5aa31c9f), the Trump administration is set to receive a total of $10 billion in the deal that allowed TikTok to remain in the US. The new investors who acquired stakes in the US entity of TikTok already paid a $2.5 billion fee to the administration when the deal closed in January, but _WSJ_'s latest report noted that the group of investors would continue to make payments until the total hits $10 billion.

After a group of investors, which includes Oracle along with the [Silver Lake](https://www.engadget.com/ea-confirms-it-will-go-private-in-55-billion-acquisition-133841614.html) and MGX investment firms, acquired stakes in the US-based TikTok entity called TikTok USDS Joint Venture, the [_WSJ_](https://www.wsj.com/business/deals/u-s-government-expected-to-get-multibillion-dollar-fee-in-tiktok-deal-685e3944) previously reported that the administration would receive a "multibillion-dollar fee" for its work on the deal. To better contextualize the recently-revealed $10 billion fee the Trump administration is receiving, the US entity of TikTok was valued at $14 billion by Vice President JD Vance.

The Trump administration has previously involved itself in major deals with other US corporations. Last year, the administration invested [$8.9 billion](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/trumps-administration-may-look-to-buy-a-stake-in-intel-213234862.html) into Intel and received a nearly 9 percent equity stake. In terms of unprecedented windfalls, the Trump administration also received a [Boeing 747-8](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/21/nx-s1-5406420/trump-accepts-qatar-plane-air-force-one) as a gift from the Qatari government in May.
