DeepSeek has [released](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=34e37b9c-8975-48da-aa39-df8bcd5badc3&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=65da9780-9d11-431f-a028-f8fb60a654d4&featureId=text-link&merchantName=CNBC&linkText=released&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5jbmJjLmNvbS8yMDI2LzA0LzI0L2RlZXBzZWVrLXY0LWxsbS1wcmV2aWV3LW9wZW4tc291cmNlLWFpLWNvbXBldGl0aW9uLWNoaW5hLmh0bWwiLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6IjY1ZGE5NzgwLTlkMTEtNDMxZi1hMDI4LWY4ZmI2MGE2NTRkNCIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY25iYy5jb20vMjAyNi8wNC8yNC9kZWVwc2Vlay12NC1sbG0tcHJldmlldy1vcGVuLXNvdXJjZS1haS1jb21wZXRpdGlvbi1jaGluYS5odG1sIn0&signature=AQAAAQPuDoUJeD7NawMblHLh90U7nYUy-cImN2riobwPkK-t&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cnbc.com%2F2026%2F04%2F24%2Fdeepseek-v4-llm-preview-open-source-ai-competition-china.html) its latest AI models, the V4 Pro and Flash versions, a bit over a year after it went viral and [became the top rated free app](https://www.engadget.com/ai/chinas-deepseek-ai-assistant-becomes-top-free-iphone-app-as-us-tech-stocks-take-a-hit-134445151.html) on Apple's App Store in the US. “Welcome to the era of cost-effective 1 million context length,” DeepSeek said in its [announcement](https://x.com/deepseek_ai/status/2047516922263285776). Context length is what you call the maximum number of tokens that an AI model can remember, so the bigger it is, the more coherent and consistent an AI is when it comes to extended conversations. [OpenAI’s recently announced GPT‑5.5](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/) has a context window ranging from 400,000 to 1 million, for instance.

The new model is still open-source, allowing users to download its code and modify it if they want. DeepSeek says V4 Pro has enhanced agentic capabilities and claims that it rivals top closed-source models when it comes to reasoning. It also says that it trails only Gemini-3.1-Pro in rich world knowledge. Meanwhile, V4 Flash isn’t quite as powerful as the V4 Pro, but it has faster response times. Still, its reasoning abilities closely approach V4 Pro, DeepSeek says, and it performs on par with with the Pro version on simple Agent tasks.

Shortly after DeepSeek topped the App Store charts, it was [banned](https://www.engadget.com/ai/us-lawmakers-want-deepseek-banned-from-government-devices-212230100.html) for use by US federal agencies and on government-owned devices. Authorities believed it was a national security risk and posed a threat to US AI stocks. South Korea also [paused](https://www.engadget.com/ai/south-korea-pauses-downloads-of-deepseek-ai-over-privacy-concerns-163302959.html) downloads of its app over privacy concerns.
