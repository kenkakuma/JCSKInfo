Capcom [revealed](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MLW4zwDAy7A) during its March 5 Spotlight showcase that _Pragmata_, its [repeatedly delayed](https://www.engadget.com/capcoms-pragmata-has-been-delayed-for-a-second-time-002035406.html) dystopian sci-fi adventure game, will release on April 17 for PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, Nintendo Switch 2 and PC via Steam. The game had been set for April 24 since its appearance at [The Game Awards](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/capcoms-pragmata-is-coming-your-way-on-april-24-010826258.html) in December.

The game’s [new trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uSjX4NCGfgM&t=9s) shows previously unseen locations and gameplay moments, and new elements within the Shelter, the in-game lunar base. _Pragmata_ was [first revealed](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xoxCboik0Is) in 2020 with a 2022 release window. Capcom then [delayed](https://news.capcomusa.com/lets/browse/an-update-on-the-development-of-pragmata) it to 2023, then went radio silent on the project before resurfacing with a [new 2026 date](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/capcoms-pragmata-is-coming-your-way-on-april-24-010826258.html) last year.

A free Sketchbook demo is available now on the PlayStation Store, Xbox Store, Nintendo eShop and Steam. Pre-orders for the game are available now.
