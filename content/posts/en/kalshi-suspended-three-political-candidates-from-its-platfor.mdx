Prediction market Kalshi has taken action [against](https://news.kalshi.com/p/kalshi-political-insider-trading-enforcement-update) three political candidates, alleging that each was engaged with insider trading of information about their campaigns. The company implemented [new rules](https://news.kalshi.com/p/kalshi-new-guardrails-insider-trading-politics-sports) last month aimed at preventing politicians and athletes from placing bets on events they can control, and it said those guardrails helped to flag this trio of cases.  

The three candidates are Mark Moran of Virginia, Matt Klein of Minnesota and Ezekiel Enriquez of Texas. Kalshi reached settlements with [Klein](https://kalshi-public-docs.s3.amazonaws.com/regulatory/notices/KDA20260005%20Klein%20Notice%20of%20Settlement%20\(4.21.2026\)%20\(7\).pdf) and [Enriquez](https://kalshi-public-docs.s3.amazonaws.com/regulatory/notices/KDA20260004%20Enriquez%20-%20Notice%20of%20Settlement%20\(4.21.2025\).pdf), both of whom cooperated in the platform's investigations. Each will face a fine of less than $1,000 and suspensions of up to five years. Moran's case has resulted in a [disciplinary action](https://kalshi-public-docs.s3.amazonaws.com/regulatory/notices/KDA20260003%20Moran%20Notice%20of%20Disciplinary%20Action.pdf), with a five year suspension and a fine of more than $6,000. He [posted](https://x.com/itsmarkmoran/status/2047038838699446324?s=20) on X about the situation and claimed this was essentially a stunt to see if he'd be caught and "to highlight how this company is destroying young men."

Kalshi and other prediction markets have been the subject of several lawsuits by state attorneys general that are attempting to regulate the sector as gambling. [Nevada](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/nevada-sues-kalshi-for-operating-a-sports-gambling-market-without-a-license-175721982.html), [Arizona](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/arizona-attorney-general-sues-kalshi-on-illegal-gambling-charges-172006290.html) and [New York](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/new-york-attorney-general-sues-two-prediction-markets-on-illegal-gambling-allegations-192012225.html) have cases underway, but the state-level attempts are not looking promising. An appeals court ruled against [New Jersey's effort](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/new-jersey-has-no-right-to-ban-kalshis-prediction-market-us-appeals-court-rules-214448284.html) to govern this industry, and the US Commodity Futures Trading Commission has launched a [lawsuit](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/cftc-sues-three-states-for-trying-to-regulate-prediction-markets-190152226.html) of its own in an effort to ensure it will be the only party to regulate prediction markets.
