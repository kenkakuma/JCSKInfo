Don’t expect the Steam Deck to be easier to get anytime soon. Valve has [posted](https://www.reddit.com/r/SteamDeck/comments/1r6s567/update_valve_is_aware_that_steam_decks_are_out_of/) a notice on the [Steam Deck page](https://store.steampowered.com/steamdeck/) with a warning that the handheld gaming console “may be out of stock intermittently” in certain regions “due to memory and storage shortages.” The company also reiterated that the more affordable Steam Deck LCD is no longer in production and will no longer be available once stocks run out. Valve started [phasing out](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/valve-discontinued-the-last-remaining-lcd-model-of-the-steam-deck-171548195.html) the LCD console back in December, which means the OLED handhelds are now the only choice for gamers who want to get a Steam Deck. The company’s notice comes after it completely [ran out of Steam Deck units](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/the-great-ramaggedon-of-2026-might-have-just-claimed-the-steam-deck-211958306.html) a few days ago.

RAM and storage shortages are plaguing tech manufacturers due to massive demand for those components from the artificial intelligence industry. AI companies have been snapping up available memory chips and hard drives for their rapid infrastructure buildouts, leaving everyone else short. In fact, we [couldn’t find any deals](https://www.engadget.com/ai/there-arent-any-black-friday-deals-on-ram-this-year-and-you-can-thank-ai-for-that-130000335.html) for RAM last Black Friday, and Samsung global marketing leader Wonjin Lee [warned](https://www.engadget.com/ai/samsung-says-ram-costs-will-likely-lead-to-price-hikes-soon-170653524.html) at CES 2026 that memory price hikes are on the horizon.

Valve also had to [delay](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/valve-pushes-back-steam-machine-launch-due-to-storage-and-memory-shortage-133000103.html) the release of the Steam Machine and the Steam Frame VR headset due to industry-wide memory and storage shortages.
