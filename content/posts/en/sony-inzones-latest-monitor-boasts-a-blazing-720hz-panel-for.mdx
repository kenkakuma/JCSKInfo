Super fast gaming displays have grown in popularity recently following the release of [several new models back at CES](https://www.engadget.com/computing/accessories/the-gaming-monitors-that-caught-our-eye-at-ces-2026-130000433.html?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAF28k3xfmfRkWfn8bQMnUSxfDPZY9wL8OsZ7JT-dWUzdrdgmAvzix3Bz3u6VkPZFCE0LP3gq1x8bv-Ois4rQxn9jJdajrBk-pRU-tJh5ICn4rDEGc7J7lXg9sC3_g3vcz420K9rZFclmfNd5kKGq6qWMWRPoJzUOUar40FYE7jxk). Now Sony is hopping on that bandwagon with its latest display featuring a tandem OLED panel from LG that offers the choice of either 540Hz or 720Hz refresh rates. 

That said, priced at $1,100, the new 24.5-inch [Sony Inzone M10S II](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=15ca523d-d465-4d35-84bc-dfe76350e474&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=54329b3a-857d-4ce9-bfe6-c9056271cbcb&featureId=text-link&merchantName=Sony+Electronics&linkText=Sony+Inzone+M10S+II&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL2VsZWN0cm9uaWNzLnNvbnkuY29tL3R2LXZpZGVvL2dhbWluZy1tb25pdG9ycy9hbGwtaW56b25lLW1vbml0b3JzL3Avc2RtMjdxMTAyLWIiLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6IjU0MzI5YjNhLTg1N2QtNGNlOS1iZmU2LWM5MDU2MjcxY2JjYiIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9lbGVjdHJvbmljcy5zb255LmNvbS90di12aWRlby9nYW1pbmctbW9uaXRvcnMvYWxsLWluem9uZS1tb25pdG9ycy9wL3NkbTI3cTEwMi1iIn0&signature=AQAAAbVIhQUEzqtYIuYzAIeTqy1a4LK9oTiVilAxHWgpMCnF&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Felectronics.sony.com%2Ftv-video%2Fgaming-monitors%2Fall-inzone-monitors%2Fp%2Fsdm27q102-b) is only for the most dedicated and deep-pocketed gamers. In normal use, the monitor offers a 540Hz refresh rate at QHD (2,560 x 1,440). However, in competitive situations where that still might not be enough, the display can go even faster by reducing its resolution to 720p while boosting its refresh rate all the way up to 720Hz. On top of that, to help make visuals clearer, Sony added a new Motion Blur Reduction algorithm with Black Frame Insertion that boosts brightness while still delivering a response rate of just 0.02ms.

Unfortunately, at $1,100, this thing is out of reach for most gamers.

Sony

Elsewhere, a new Super Anti-Glare film helps reduce reflections. The company also used feedback from pro gamers to create a stand with a smaller footprint and a wider range of tilt adjustability (from -5 to 35 degrees). For those worried about the panel's long-term performance, the Inzone M10S II comes with a three-year warranty and OLED protection features like a custom heat sink.

Sony

Not only is it really light at just 199 grams (not including its detachable cable and boom mic), it also features a dedicated RPG/Adventure profile designed to improve clarity and environmental details. The one caveat is that to access this mode, you need to use Sony's USB-C Audio box, which offers additional features like virtual 7.1 surround sound and support for 360-degree spatial audio.

Finally, while they aren't brand new, Sony is releasing a translucent Glass Purple version of its [Inzone wireless gaming earbuds](https://www.engadget.com/sony-inzone-buds-hands-on-a-slick-gamer-focused-twist-on-the-wf-1000xm5-specs-price-160014839.html) that conjures up nostalgic memories of [Nintendo’s Atomic Purple N64](https://throwbackgaming.com/cdn/shop/files/s-l1600_2_f8c3dab1-350e-4773-b37a-4ce716bc10a9.jpg?v=1744484649&width=1800), along with Fnatic Editions of its [Mouse-A, Mat-F and Mat-D](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/sony-inzone-launches-new-peripherals-in-collaboration-with-fnatic-160037799.html) peripherals.

All of Sony's new gadgets are [available today](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/sony-electronics-expands-gaming-gear-lineup-with-inzone-h6-air-and-inzone-m10s-ii-302741559.html), aside from the Inzone M10S II monitor, which is due out sometime later this year.
