Workers at Heart Machine, the independent studio behind _Hyper Light Drifter_ and _Solar Ash_, have [formed a union](https://cwa-union.org/news/releases/video-game-developers-heart-machine-secure-voluntary-union-recognition-cwa) with Communications Workers of America (CWA) Local 9003. The wall-to-wall unit covers all [13 frontline employees](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/-if-we-love-this-work-we-have-to-protect-it-heart-machine-workers-secure-voluntary-union-recognition) at the studio, which [voluntarily recognized](https://www.dol.gov/sites/dolgov/files/general/workcenter/Neutrality-Guidance.pdf) the union in February after a supermajority of eligible workers voted for the measure.

The organizing effort follows a rough stretch at Heart Machine, after the studio [laid off](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/hyper-light-breaker-developer-heart-machine-confirms-layoffs) employees in November 2024, then announced in October 2025 that it would [end development](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/hyper-light-breaker-studio-will-end-the-games-development-and-lay-off-staff-162941243.html) on its early access title _Hyper Light Breaker_ and cut further staff.

"I decided to get involved in organizing my studio because I've seen so many peers in the industry stand up to protect the craft we all care so deeply about. Watching that momentum grow made me realize that if we love this work, we have to protect it, especially now," said Steph Aligbe, a gameplay tools engineer at the studio.

Heart Machine joining the CWA extends the union's gaming footprint even further. The union counts [thousands of employees](https://cwa-union.org/news/nothing-stops-blizzard-cwa-video-game-workers-ratify-third-microsoft-contract) at Microsoft subsidiaries among its members, as well as [staff at EA](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/unionized-ea-staffers-are-not-happy-about-that-proposed-saudi-backed-acquisition-155559256.html), [Id Software](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/doom-studio-id-software-forms-wall-to-wall-union-with-a-majority-of-employees-voting-in-favor-164808829.html) and others. CWA also runs the [United Videogame Workers](https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/video-game-workers-in-north-america-now-have-an-industry-wide-union-130024730.html), a direct-join union that launched in 2025, allowing individual game workers in the US and Canada to sign up on their own without elections or employer consent. Large gaming studios like Ubisoft have been undergoing a [seemingly](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/star-wars-outlaws-developer-massive-entertainment-and-ubisoft-stockholm-face-layoffs-134234968.html) [endless](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/ubisoft-proposes-even-more-layoffs-after-last-weeks-studio-closures-and-game-cancellations-192703241.html) string of [layoffs](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/ubisoft-lays-off-40-staff-working-on-splinter-cell-remake-says-game-remains-in-development-151139753.html), and workers are increasingly demanding to have [their voices heard](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/1200-ubisoft-workers-went-on-strike-in-response-to-company-restructuring-and-mandatory-return-to-work-policy-163714986.html).
