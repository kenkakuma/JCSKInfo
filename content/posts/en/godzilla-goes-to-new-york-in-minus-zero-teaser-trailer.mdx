Japanese entertainment company Toho has [released](https://godzilla.com/pages/godzilla-minus-zero?srsltid=AfmBOooOQBbDw5OTAjTzQPkcdWGBlTOnhhxv2Flz5vHjXDGJwdikX8J1) a teaser video for _Godzilla Minus Zero_, the upcoming sequel to the award-winning film [_Godzilla Minus One_](https://www.engadget.com/you-can-now-watch-godzilla-minus-one-at-home-on-netflix-194759870.html). The teaser shows the famous monster next to the Statue of Liberty as it rampages across New York. _Godzilla Minus Zero_ is set in 1949, two years after the events of the first film, and will be a direct sequel. You’ll see familiar faces from _Minus One_ in the short trailer, as well, namely Koichi Shikishima and Noriko Oishi, two of the first movie’s main characters.

The kaiju flick was filmed specifically for IMAX with high-definition digital cameras. Even its audio was optimized for the massive screen’s immersive cinema experience. _Minus One_ won an Oscar for Best Visual Effects, so expectations are high for this sequel. The good news is that this movie is also helmed by Takashi Yamazaki, who wrote, directed and oversaw the visual effects for _Minus One_. _Godzilla Minus Zero_ is heading to cinemas in Japan on November 3 and in the United States on November 6 this year.
