Josh Wardle had a hell of a tough act to follow. His last game, _Wordle_, became [incredibly popular](https://www.engadget.com/wordle-new-york-times-millions-new-users-151308110.html) thanks to its blend of simplicity, clarity and shareability. Over four years (and a seven-figure [sale of _Wordle_ to _The New York Times_](https://www.engadget.com/wordle-nyt-new-york-times-sale-215510156-215510216.html)) later, Wardle has released his follow-up game. 

Like _Wordle_ and its myriad clones, [_Parseword_](https://www.parseword.com/) offers up one puzzle per day. It taps into the mechanics of cryptic crossword clues. So it might ask you to replace one of the words with a synonym, reverse it or find a homophone. You may need to remove letters from a word or combine it with another one to find the answer. There’s a tutorial to help you get to grips with things and hints are available. 

Wardle told [_The New Yorker_](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=2c74dc58-9aed-4251-a9fd-8b3521347943&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=d342384b-4f79-4a3c-8dcb-2db1a4bbcd91&featureId=text-link&merchantName=The+New+Yorker&linkText=The+New+Yorker&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5uZXd5b3JrZXIuY29tL2N1bHR1cmUvdGhlLWxlZGUvdGhlLWNyZWF0b3Itb2Ytd29yZGxlLXRyaWVzLXRvLXNvbHZlLXRoZS1jcnlwdGljLWNyb3Nzd29yZCIsImNvbnRlbnRVdWlkIjoiZDM0MjM4NGItNGY3OS00YTNjLThkY2ItMmRiMWE0YmJjZDkxIiwib3JpZ2luYWxVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5uZXd5b3JrZXIuY29tL2N1bHR1cmUvdGhlLWxlZGUvdGhlLWNyZWF0b3Itb2Ytd29yZGxlLXRyaWVzLXRvLXNvbHZlLXRoZS1jcnlwdGljLWNyb3Nzd29yZCJ9&signature=AQAAATpHk5u_21GarfMhyueGJ_9SkDS2i2nNB2EidWKI2JE6&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.newyorker.com%2Fculture%2Fthe-lede%2Fthe-creator-of-wordle-tries-to-solve-the-cryptic-crossword) he was inspired by _The Last of Us_ showrunner [Craig Mazin](https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/hbo-is-making-a-baldurs-gate-3-tv-show-130000864.html), who he heard discuss the logic behind cryptics on a podcast. Wardle brought in two friends, Chris Dary and Matt Lee, whom he worked with at Reddit to collaborate on the new game. They received permission to use clues from long-time cryptic constructors Emily Cox and Henry Rathvon. 

After playing the tutorial and today's puzzle, it seems you may need to be wired in a certain way to play _Parseword_. It’s certainly less approachable than _Wordle_. At first glance, it’s hard to see this reaching the same kind of mass appeal as Wardle’s previous game. (Fittingly enough, _Parseword_ feels like a game geared toward _New Yorker_ readers.)

Of course, countless other casual daily games that aim to replicate the success of _Wordle_ have [popped up](https://www.engadget.com/wordle-clones-have-jumped-the-shark-123231290.html) over the last few years, from _Quordle_, [_Bracket City_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/bracket-city-could-be-your-latest-word-game-obsession-151405964.html) and _Framed_ to the _Times_’ own [_Connections_](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/nyt-connections-bot-uses-ai-to-highlight-each-days-top-mistakes-145242525.html)_, Strands_ and _Pips_. [_Catfishing_](https://catfishing.net/), which asks you to guess Wikipedia article subjects based on their categories, is a new favorite of mine. I still miss the original [_Heardle_](https://www.engadget.com/spotify-will-shut-down-heardle-on-may-5th-151454849.html), though.
