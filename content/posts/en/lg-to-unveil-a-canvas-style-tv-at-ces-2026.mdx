LG will unveil a canvas-style art TV, dubbed the LG Gallery TV, at CES 2026. The new model will be offered in 55-inch and 65-inch variants, and sports a flush-mount design along with customizable magnetic frames.

The Gallery TV uses a Mini LED display and the company's [Alpha 7](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=472a467b-5d76-4e49-b1e8-d1e77258dd8e&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=015dd3e2-d5eb-49dd-8975-01f44c28af7f&featureId=text-link&merchantName=LG+Electronics&linkText=Alpha+7&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5sZy5jb20vaW4vb2xlZC10dnMvMjAyMS9hNy1haS1wcm9jZXNzb3IvP3Nyc2x0aWQ9QWZtQk9vcG1DTWJ2UFg2ai1PYlFzSlRGZXlHWFhWcXhKczhaV1Vzc0syX0xjaFpkNmxObER5UmoiLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6IjAxNWRkM2UyLWQ1ZWItNDlkZC04OTc1LTAxZjQ0YzI4YWY3ZiIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubGcuY29tL2luL29sZWQtdHZzLzIwMjEvYTctYWktcHJvY2Vzc29yLz9zcnNsdGlkPUFmbUJPb3BtQ01idlBYNmotT2JRc0pURmV5R1hYVnF4SnM4WldVc3NLMl9MY2haZDZsTmxEeVJqIn0&signature=AQAAAWnwS3YjGy57HInMBw5rfkDTvto7tRIrhhZEcszBrGwc&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.lg.com%2Fin%2Foled-tvs%2F2021%2Fa7-ai-processor%2F%3Fsrsltid%3DAfmBOopmCMbvPX6j-ObQsJTFeyGXXVqxJs8ZWUssK2_LchZd6lNlDyRj) AI processor and offers 4K resolution. The new model will also leverage the LG Gallery+ service, a paid subscription with a library of over 4,500 works that users can display on the TV. Users will also be able to create custom images using generative AI or display images from personal photo libraries.

LG says the TV was developed with museum curators, and will feature a Gallery Mode that optimizes brightness and color to show off the texture of displayed artwork. The display will have some degree of reflection handling and glare reduction, though precise details were not shared. The TV will automatically adjust picture settings to maintain an optimal image in response to changing ambient light throughout the day.

This isn't the first time LG has released an art-inspired TV. It released an ultra-thin OLED model called the [LG GX Gallery TV](https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=472a467b-5d76-4e49-b1e8-d1e77258dd8e&siteId=us-engadget&pageId=1p-autolink&contentUuid=015dd3e2-d5eb-49dd-8975-01f44c28af7f&featureId=text-link&merchantName=LG+Electronics&linkText=LG+GX+Gallery+TV&custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5sZy5jb20vZWFzdGFmcmljYS9sZ29sZWQvZ3gtZ2FsbGVyeSIsImNvbnRlbnRVdWlkIjoiMDE1ZGQzZTItZDVlYi00OWRkLTg5NzUtMDFmNDRjMjhhZjdmIiwib3JpZ2luYWxVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5sZy5jb20vZWFzdGFmcmljYS9sZ29sZWQvZ3gtZ2FsbGVyeSJ9&signature=AQAAAejS0iwChPQbnYsQYnN7JaUf6Wys0-9swXQgR6Urf8dR&gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.lg.com%2Feastafrica%2Flgoled%2Fgx-gallery) in 2020. It has also released other "Gallery Design" TVs that offer wall-flush mounting in the past, but the new LG Gallery TV with dedicated art-focused features seems like a more direct competitor to [Samsung's "The Frame"](https://www.engadget.com/home/home-theater/samsung-the-frame-pro-at-ces-2025-a-big-upgrade-for-the-art-tv-214300273.html) or the [Hisense CanvasTV](https://www.hisense-usa.com/product-page/televisions-65-class-s7-canvastv-4k-qled-google-tv-65s7n).
