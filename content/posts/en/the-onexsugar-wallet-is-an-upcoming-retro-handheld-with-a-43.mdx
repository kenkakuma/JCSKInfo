OneXPlayer is quickly establishing itself as a company that isn't afraid to get weird as hell. (Take, for example, its [transforming dual-screen gaming portable](https://www.engadget.com/gaming/next-gen-snapdragon-g-series-chips-will-power-handhelds-from-ayaneo-onexsugar-and-retroid-pocket-131733930.html).) Its latest venture is another retro gaming handheld that, at first glance, looks like a standard dual-screen model. But the Android-powered OneXSugar Wallet uses a single foldable screen instead.

The device was teased via a [54-second video](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1eyvWBVE3y/) on the Chinese video-sharing platform Bilibili. _Retro Handhelds_ [reports](https://retrohandhelds.gg/onexsugar-wallet-is-first-android-handheld-with-a-foldable-screen/) that the Wallet uses an 8.01-inch OLED with a 2,480 x 1,860 resolution. (That's a 4:3 aspect ratio when unfolded.) The video also shows an asymmetrical analog stick layout with a D-pad and four action buttons.
